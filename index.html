What is plagiarism? And What are the levels of plagiarism?

Plagiarism is presenting someone else's work or ideas as your own, with or without their consent, by incorporating it into your work without full acknowledgement.

What Are The Types Of Plagiarism?

If a writer copies, word for word, the text from the author book and doesn't acknowledge in anyway, that it was author word for word work then we will name this type of the plagiarism (copying).
When the plagiarizer borrows the "phrases and clauses from the original source and weaves them into his own writing" without putting the phrases in quotation marks or citing the author then we will name this type of the plagiarism (Patchwork Plagiarism).
The plagiarizer paraphrases or summarizes another's work without citing the source then we will name this type of the plagiarism (Paraphrasing Plagiarism).
The writer incorrectly quotes and/or incorrectly cites a source they are using then we will name this type of the plagiarism (Unintentional).

What is the importance of plagiarism detection?

Whether you know it or not, plagiarism does affect you in some way. It doesn't matter whether you're the content creator or the one who consumes the content, plagiarism affects us all. As a content creator (writer, author, researcher, student, freelancer, blogger, etc. It's no news that you should be regularly checking to be sure that nobody is copying your content without your permission.

Can you go to jail if you plagiarize?

Most cases of plagiarism are considered misdemeanors, punishable by fines of anywhere between $100 and $50,000 — and up to one year in jail. Plagiarism can also be considered a felony under certain state and federal laws.


Why is it important to not plagiarize?

Plagiarism is unethical because it is a form of theft. By taking the ideas and words of others and pretending they are your own, you are stealing someone else's intellectual property.

What happens to students who plagiarize?

If you unintentionally plagiarize, and you have no previous infractions, then most colleges will lower your grade or fail you for the course.
